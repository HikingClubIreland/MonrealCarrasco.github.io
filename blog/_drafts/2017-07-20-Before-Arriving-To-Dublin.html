--- layout: post title: "Before Arriving To Dublin" category: "Life-In-Dublin" tags: [dublin] date: "2017-07-20 22:16:51 +0100" --- If you are planning to come to Dublin, Ireland, for study purposes there are a few things you need to take into consideration:
  • Weather:

    The seasons in Dublin always come with rain and wind, even in summer! You get the 4 seasons in one day, yes, there's no mistake you just read 4 seasons in 1 day!!!

  • Acommodation:

    It is quite hard but not impossible to secure an accommodation in Dublin. Many students tend to share accommodation, but be aware, they live 4-5 in one bedroom, and some houses have 15+ students living in 3-4 bedrooms.

    Another option is to rent your own flat/studio/apartment/house, if you have the enough funding to pay for it, it shouldn't be a problem. In theory if you live far away from the City Centre it's cheaper, but the reality in most cases is that it is the same or even more expensive because of transportation.

    I would suggest to start looking for accommodation in sites listed below, and you might even add some alerts to get notifications to your e-mail. DO NOT TRY TO SECURE ACCOMMODATION FROM YOUR COUNTRY!!! There are too many scams and you must see the places first before paying for them. There are also Facebook Groups where you may find accommodation advertised by people moving from the place, just be aware of scams like I said before.

    You might find it difficult, because it is, but do not worry you will find a place to live, just do not say yes to the first just because you are scared you will not find something. When going to view a place, do not go with many people, landlords do not like party animals, and might confuse you with one. The viewings usually are open viewings, this mean there will be at least 30 more people trying to secure that accommodation, try not to feel the pressure. Be the first to call, to e-mail and to arrive to the place, show that you are interested but not desesperated.

    Go to your service provider and ask them to unlock it. They might refuse to do it, but you can put in a service complaint in the SUBTEL and they will help you with the issue.

After you have completed checking all of the above, you need to start preparing for your trip. First of all, everything will be just fine. It might seem like you are going to be far away from home, from your friends and family, but with the technology we currently have you will be able to talk to them everyday, you will be able to keep in touch with them without a problem.
